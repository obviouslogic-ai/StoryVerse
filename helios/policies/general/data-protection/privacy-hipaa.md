# Privacy and Data Protection Policy

## Purpose
Define requirements for use, disclosure, and protection of personal data, protected health information (PHI), and personally identifiable information (PII). Ensure SSC does not give, rent, or sell access to customer data to any other individual, partner, or entity unless directed under lawful court order.

## Scope
All systems, personnel, and third parties that collect, access, maintain, distribute, process, protect, store, use, transmit, dispose of, or otherwise handle personal data, PII, or PHI. Any agreements with vendors must contain similar protective language.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy — Privacy
- Use and disclose PHI only for permitted purposes and with minimum necessary access.
- Honor individual rights to access, amend, and receive an accounting of disclosures of their personal data.
- Maintain privacy notices and disclosure records where required.
- Apply safeguards to prevent unauthorized use or disclosure.
- Process personal information only in a manner compatible with and relevant to the purpose for which it was collected or authorized.
- Allow users and website visitors access to their own personal information and the ability to correct, amend, or delete inaccurate information, except where the burden is disproportionate to the privacy risk or where rights of other individuals would be violated.

## Policy — Data Protection
- SSC does not give, rent, or sell access to customer data to anyone else, nor make use of it for any purpose other than to deliver SSC services.
- Take all reasonable steps to protect data received from customers and users from loss, misuse, or unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.
- Maintain physical, logical, and process controls to safeguard customer data.
- Encrypt all PII at rest and in transit using approved encryption standards.
- Any individual who suspects that a theft, breach, or exposure of SSC protected or sensitive data has occurred must immediately report it per the Incident Response Policy.

## Standards and Procedures
- Maintain a data subject request (DSR) process for access, amendment, and deletion requests.
- Document permitted disclosures and retention of disclosure logs.
- Ensure staff training on privacy obligations as part of security awareness training.
- Establish procedures for complaints and privacy inquiries.
- Conduct privacy impact assessments for new systems or significant changes to data processing.
- Review and update privacy notices at least annually.
- Maintain records of processing activities and data flows.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- Privacy Officer: owns privacy governance, compliance, and data subject request processes.
- Security Lead: ensures technical safeguards align with privacy requirements.
- Legal: advises on regulatory obligations and disclosure requirements.
- All Personnel: comply with privacy and confidentiality rules; report suspected data exposure immediately.

## Enforcement
- Any SSC personnel found in violation of this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Third-party partners in violation may have their network connection terminated.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon regulatory changes.
- Exceptions require documented legal review and approval.
