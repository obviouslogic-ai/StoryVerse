# Workforce Security and Security Awareness Policy

## Purpose
Establish workforce security controls, mandatory security training, and remote work security requirements to protect SSC information assets across all work environments.

## Scope
All employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third-party personnel performing work on behalf of SSC.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy — Personnel Security
- Perform background checks consistent with role requirements and local law, including: criminal record search (statewide and national), employment verification, education verification (highest post-secondary degree), and OFAC screening where applicable.
- Require signed confidentiality agreements and acceptable use acknowledgment prior to granting access to SSC systems or data.
- Require employment agreements prior to system access where applicable.
- Enforce least privilege access and timely access removal upon separation.
- Ensure asset return and access revocation procedures are completed during offboarding.
- Employees must be insured, including insurance covering risks arising from performance of services.

## Policy — Security Awareness Training
- Provide security awareness training at onboarding for all new employees and contractors.
- Conduct annual security awareness refresher training for all staff.
- Conduct quarterly security-focused training sessions for IT and engineering teams covering current threats, incident response, and operational security.
- Training covers: security policies, acceptable use, password management, phishing recognition, data handling, incident reporting, privacy obligations, and AI usage guidelines.
- Maintain records of training completion and policy acknowledgments.
- Provide role-based training for personnel with access to regulated data (PHI, PII).

## Policy — Remote Work Security
- Remote workers must maintain an appropriate and secure work environment including:
  - A private workspace free from external noise and unauthorized access.
  - A password-protected Wi-Fi network with minimum 25 Mbps/10 Mbps internet speed.
  - Use of the company-provided VPN when accessing client PII or internal systems.
  - Locked file cabinets and desks for physical documents.
  - Computer must be locked before leaving the workspace.
- In the event of a home network outage, an alternative location may be used to maintain connectivity.
- SSC is not responsible for costs associated with the setup of the employee's home office (remodeling, furniture, lighting, repairs).
- SSC-supplied equipment is for business purposes only; employees must sign an inventory of all SSC property received.
- Upon termination, all company property must be returned to SSC.
- Equipment maintenance: SSC-supplied equipment is maintained by SSC; employee-owned equipment (if approved) is maintained by the employee.

## Standards and Procedures
- Document disciplinary procedures for policy violations.
- Ensure personnel are notified of security roles and responsibilities at onboarding.
- Maintain training completion records and schedule compliance reminders.
- Review training content annually and update for emerging threats and regulatory changes.
- SSC reserves the right to determine appropriate equipment needs for each remote work arrangement.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- HR: manages onboarding, background checks, training records, and offboarding procedures.
- Security Lead: defines training content, cadence, and remote work security requirements.
- Managers: ensure team compliance with training requirements and remote work policies.
- All Personnel: complete required training, safeguard assigned equipment, and maintain secure workspaces.

## Enforcement
- Employees found in violation of this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon regulatory changes.
- Exceptions require documented approval through the Policy Exception Management process.
