# Artificial Intelligence Usage Policy

## Purpose
Establish baseline requirements for the acceptable use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and services to protect company data, customer data, and intellectual property.

## Scope
All SSC employees and affiliates, including remote workers, contractors, and third parties with access to SSC data or systems. Applies to all AI tools and services, including but not limited to large language models, image generators, and AI-powered assistants.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy
- No Personally Identifiable Information (PII) may be entered into any AI tool for any reason. This includes internal employee data, applicant data, and customer data.
- Data related to sales, marketing, IT, engineering, code, finances, accounting, customers, partners, or contracts must not be entered into external AI tools or services unless the tool has been approved by the Security Lead and appropriate data handling agreements are in place.
- All AI tools and services used for business purposes must be reviewed and approved by the Security Lead prior to use.
- Responses from AI tools must not be used as reliable sources of information for legal, medical, adjudication, or other business decisions that directly relate to the delivery of SSC services without independent verification by qualified personnel.
- AI-generated code must undergo the same peer review, security assessment, and quality assurance processes as human-written code per the Secure SDLC Policy.
- Employees must be aware that external AI services copy, store, and may use all submitted data, images, and content for their own purposes, including human review.

## Standards and Procedures
- Maintain a list of approved AI tools and services with associated usage restrictions.
- Require Security Lead approval before introducing any new AI tool into business workflows.
- Include AI usage guidelines in security awareness training.
- Review and update the list of approved AI tools at least annually.
- Report suspected misuse of AI tools involving company or customer data to the Security Lead immediately.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- Security Lead: maintains approved AI tools list, defines usage restrictions, reviews exceptions.
- Engineering: ensures AI-generated code meets secure development standards.
- All Personnel: comply with AI usage restrictions and report concerns.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon significant changes to AI technology landscape.
- Exceptions require documented approval by the Security Lead with compensating controls.
