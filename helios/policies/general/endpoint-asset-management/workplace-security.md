# Workplace Security Policy

## Purpose
Define requirements for maintaining physical security, environmental safeguards, and clean workspace practices to protect information assets, computing infrastructure, and human assets across all SSC facilities and remote work environments.

## Scope
All SSC office locations, data processing facilities, remote work environments, and all employees, contractors, trainees, visitors, and third parties who access SSC premises.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy — Physical Security
- Establish security perimeters to protect areas containing information systems and prevent unauthorized physical access, damage, and interference.
- Maintain a list of personnel with authorized physical access to facilities where information systems reside; review and approve access lists periodically.
- Control all physical access points (including designated entry/exit points) and grant access only after verification of authorization.
- Monitor physical access to detect and respond to physical security incidents.
- Apply physical protection against damage from fire, flood, earthquake, explosion, civil unrest, and other natural or man-made disasters.
- Position information systems within facilities to minimize risk from physical and environmental hazards and opportunities for unauthorized access.
- Protect information systems from power failure and other disruptions caused by failure in supporting utilities.
- Protect power and telecommunications cabling from interception or damage.
- Monitor real-time physical intrusion alarm and surveillance equipment.

## Policy — Office Security
- SSC corporate offices are secured with 24-hour video surveillance, badge access systems, physical locks, and visitor sign-in logs.
- Corporate offices do not house infrastructure related to the SSC Cloud product; application infrastructure resides in the cloud.
- Visitors must be escorted by designated personnel, and visitor activities must be monitored.
- Maintain a record of all physical access by both visitors and authorized individuals.
- Authenticate visitors before authorizing access to non-public areas.

## Policy — Clean Desk
- Remove all sensitive and confidential materials from workspaces and secure them in locked storage at the end of the day or when the workspace is unoccupied for an extended period.
- Lock computer workstations when the workspace is unoccupied.
- Shut down computer workstations completely at the end of the work day.
- Keep file cabinets containing restricted or sensitive information closed and locked when not in use.
- Do not leave keys for access to restricted information at an unattended desk.
- Lock away laptops with a locking cable or in a locked drawer when unattended.
- Do not leave passwords written down in accessible locations or on sticky notes near computers.
- Immediately remove printouts containing restricted or sensitive information from printers.
- Dispose of restricted and sensitive documents using secure shredding or locked confidential disposal bins.
- Erase whiteboards containing restricted or sensitive information after use.
- Treat mass storage devices (USB drives, external media) as sensitive and secure them in locked storage.
- Clear all printers and fax machines of papers promptly after printing.

## Standards and Procedures
- Define and maintain office access controls, visitor procedures, and access logs.
- Conduct periodic physical security assessments including walk-throughs and equipment audits.
- Document emergency procedures for facilities including evacuation routes and contact information.
- Enforce clean desk practices through periodic compliance checks.
- Ensure physical access control to information systems is independent of general facility access for higher-security areas (e.g., server rooms).
- Employ automated mechanisms where possible to recognize potential intrusion and initiate response actions.
- Evaluate security risks before sanctioning connections to external networks.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- CTO/COO: responsible for proper implementation of the Physical Security Policy.
- Operations/Facilities: maintains office security infrastructure, monitoring, and access controls.
- Security Lead: defines physical security requirements and reviews compliance.
- Managers: ensure team compliance with workspace safeguards and clean desk practices.
- All Personnel: adhere to clean desk policy and physical security requirements.

## Enforcement
- Employees found in violation of this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon material changes to facilities or security posture.
- Exceptions require documented approval by the Security Lead.
