# Asset Management and Acceptable Use Policy

## Purpose
Define acceptable use of organizational IT assets and ensure assets are tracked, protected, and used responsibly. This policy protects SSC employees, partners, and the company from illegal or damaging actions while maintaining a culture of openness, trust, and integrity.

## Scope
All SSC employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and third-party affiliates. Applies to all company-owned or leased devices, accounts, software, cloud resources, and network infrastructure used to conduct SSC business or interact with internal networks and business systems.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy — Asset Management
- Maintain an inventory of all physical and virtual assets with unique identifiers and assigned owners.
- Track asset lifecycle from procurement through disposal.
- Enforce secure configuration and patching for all managed devices.
- Ensure asset disposal follows the Data Retention and Disposal Policy and data destruction requirements.
- SSC proprietary information stored on electronic and computing devices remains the sole property of SSC.

## Policy — General Use
- Employees are responsible for exercising good judgment regarding personal use of company resources.
- Promptly report the theft, loss, or unauthorized disclosure of SSC proprietary information.
- Access, use, or share SSC proprietary information only to the extent authorized and necessary for assigned job duties.
- All computing devices must be secured with a password-protected screensaver with automatic activation set to 10 minutes or less. Lock the screen or log off when the device is unattended.
- SSC reserves the right to audit networks and systems periodically to ensure compliance with this policy.

## Policy — Prohibited Activities
The following activities are prohibited on SSC resources:
- Any activity that is illegal under local, state, federal, or international law.
- Violations of intellectual property rights including installation of pirated or unlicensed software.
- Accessing data, servers, or accounts for any purpose other than conducting SSC business.
- Exporting software or technical information in violation of export control laws.
- Introduction of malicious programs (viruses, worms, Trojan horses, etc.) into the network.
- Revealing account passwords or allowing others to use your account, including family members.
- Effecting security breaches or disruptions of network communication (network sniffing, packet spoofing, denial of service, forged routing).
- Port scanning or security scanning without prior notification to the Security Lead.
- Executing network monitoring to intercept data not intended for the employee's host (unless part of normal duties).
- Circumventing user authentication or security controls.
- Providing employee information or lists to parties outside SSC without authorization.

## Policy — Email and Communications
- Do not send unsolicited bulk email (spam) or chain letters.
- Do not engage in any form of harassment via email, telephone, or messaging.
- Do not use unauthorized or forged email headers.
- Postings from an SSC email address to social media should be reviewed with your manager and include a disclaimer that opinions are the employee's own.

## Policy — Blogging and Social Media
- Limited and occasional use of SSC systems for social media is acceptable provided it is professional, does not violate policy, and does not interfere with work duties.
- SSC confidential information, trade secrets, or proprietary material must not be shared on social media.
- Do not engage in blogging that may harm SSC's image, reputation, or goodwill.
- Do not represent personal opinions as SSC positions unless authorized.
- SSC trademarks, logos, and intellectual property may not be used in personal social media without authorization.

## Standards and Procedures
- Track assets with unique identifiers, owners, and classifications.
- Enforce endpoint security controls for managed devices per the Endpoint Management Policy.
- Prohibit unauthorized software installation (all software requests require manager approval and IT review).
- Define return procedures for employee offboarding including all SSC-owned equipment and access.
- Monitor systems and network traffic as needed for security and compliance.
- Maintain audit trails for asset attachment and network access.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- IT/Operations: maintains asset inventory, enforces controls, and monitors compliance.
- Security Lead: defines acceptable use requirements and reviews violations.
- Managers: ensure team compliance with acceptable use standards.
- All Personnel: comply with asset usage rules and report policy violations.

## Enforcement
- Any SSC employee, contractor, or vendor found in violation of this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
- Third-party partners found in violation may have their network connection terminated.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon asset program changes.
- Exceptions require documented approval by the Security Lead or a member of the executive team.
