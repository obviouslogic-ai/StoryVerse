# Governance Program Policy

## Purpose
Define SSC's information governance program including oversight, accountability, policy management, and the layered security program that ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of SSC data and information assets.

## Scope
All SSC systems, processes, and personnel supporting the SSC Cloud product and corporate operations.

## Compliance Status
SSC is not currently certified under SOC 2 or HIPAA. This policy defines SSC's target controls and is maintained as part of our compliance readiness program.

## Policy
- Maintain a governance framework that defines roles, responsibilities, and accountability for information security and IT operations.
- Establish a policy lifecycle for drafting, approval, publication, review, and updates of all IT and security policies.
- Ensure governance oversight for compliance, risk, and audit activities.
- Base the information security program on the NIST SP 800-53 framework with policy prescriptions from the ITIL framework for service management.
- Maintain a layered security program comprising training, policies, procedures, standards, frameworks, and guidelines.
- The security program must ensure three vital principles: confidentiality (secured from unauthorized access), integrity (accurate and free from tampering), and availability (accessible in a timely manner).
- SSC's Risk Management Program is a key component of both technical risk and overall company risk management.
- The governance program shall: establish benchmarks for security, measure against those benchmarks, enable informed decision making, and support the execution of decisions.

## Standards and Procedures
- Maintain a policy catalog and review schedule with all policies reviewed at least annually.
- Define governance roles and responsibilities with clear accountability.
- Conduct governance reviews at least annually including assessment of policy effectiveness.
- Maintain a Security Committee with defined roles and responsibilities for security guidance, direction, and authority.
- Review the Information Security Policy at least annually.
- While not exhaustive, maintain security controls across the following layers:
  - **Infrastructure**: cloud infrastructure with regional redundancy.
  - **Process**: monthly security audits, quarterly security training, manual code review, change management, ITIL-based governance, recurring vulnerability and penetration testing.
  - **Personnel**: background screening, defined security roles and responsibilities, security awareness training.
- Maintain compliance readiness against SOC 2, HIPAA, and NIST SP 800-53 frameworks.

## Roles and Responsibilities
- Executive Sponsor / CTO: approves governance framework, risk appetite, and resource commitments.
- Compliance Lead: manages policy lifecycle, governance reporting, and internal conformance assessments.
- Security Lead: maintains security program, control oversight, and metrics reporting.
- Control Owners: adhere to policy requirements, maintain evidence of controls, and report compliance status.
- All Personnel: acknowledge and adhere to applicable policies.

## Enforcement
- Any SSC employee, contractor, or vendor found in violation of SSC policies may be subject to corrective and/or disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination.

## Review and Exceptions
- Review annually or upon significant organizational, regulatory, or technology changes.
- Policy exceptions are handled through the Policy Exception Management process.
